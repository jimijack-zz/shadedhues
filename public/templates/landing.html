About the Artist

Take a look into the evolution of ShadedHues

I started drawing with charcoal in August of 2012. I admit that I had no intention of exploring the arts before then. It was a natural chain of events I know I was meant to follow that brought me to the initial experience.

  • 2009-2012

    DRAWN-OUT DAZE

    Leading up to early 2012 I had no real sense of direction. Priorities were mis-aligned, and willingness to move forward only meant I might decide to get out of bed that day. I was given an opportunity to change the direction my life was heading. Regardless of background or opportunity, change is often met with a level of fear and uncertainty. It’s about lack of control, or the fear of the unknown. For me I was met with both of these challenges immediately. Even though I hated the way I lived my life, along with the reflection in the mirror, I didn’t know how to live any other way. It was what I was used to, and therefore, what I fooled myself into thinking I had a sense of control over. Coinciding with that ignorance, I also thought I was fooling everyone else around me. Thank God that wasn’t true…

  • January 18, 2012

    A NEW FOUND LIFE

    The last time I drank and used was the day before I moved out to Long Beach to get sober. My family (the ones I thought I was “fooling”) knew damn well what was going on with me. I had hit a hard bottom and it was time to try and pull myself out of the rabbit hole so to speak. I was too ashamed to ask for help, but not to accept it if it was offered. So that was it for me, a chance to try and get my life back was at my feet and I ran with it.

  • August 2012

    TRANSITION TO FULL-SERVICE

    I share that part of my experience because it was what directly connected me with the art world. The road to recovery is a roller-coaster ride of ups and downs as we try to gain a better understanding of not only what we did during the wreckage, but also why. This most often is met with more confusion, and instead of running back to the substance we have to face these demons head on. I happened to be sitting on my little bed in a sober living pissed off at the world trying to seek these answers when I realized I needed something to express myself. I made a decision to turn my life around and I was not going to allow the negatives to overwhelm me again to I picked up some charcoal and started drawing. After that I couldn’t put it down! Charcoal and pencil provide an inlet to connect with those unexplored portions of my soul that, up until my connection to the arts, had and would most likely have remained buried under a mountain of empty bottles and various paraphernalia.

  • Thru Today & on to Tomorrow

    PHASE 30s - EXPRESSION

    Finally I had something that could fill that void. I’m not sure why it clicked the way it did but its as if I should have been doing it the whole time! It has been an unbelievable experience these last three years finding this connection to art and expression I didn’t know I had. Its proven to be an invaluable part of my life and recovery, and I have no intentions of slowing down. Every new piece I learn something from, and each one has a story and connection I can speak for hours about. I’ve experienced a new purpose and inward connection to a piece of me I used to drown with drugs and alcohol. Call it a new freedom and a new happiness…
